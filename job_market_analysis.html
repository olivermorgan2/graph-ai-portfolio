Section 1: Introduction

This exercise uses data about jobs for Data Analysts. Using LLMs, it extracts information about the number of years of required experience and which data languages are needed. As part of the exercise, two graphs are generated.

Section 2: Experience vs. Salary Relationship

The scatter plot shows a positive correlation between years of experience and median salary, indicating that more experienced data analysts tend to command higher salaries. The trend line suggests a steady increase in salary with each additional year of experience.

Section 3: Salary Distribution by Programming Language

The box plot reveals that jobs requiring both R and Python skills tend to offer higher median salaries compared to positions requiring only one language or neither. This suggests that proficiency in multiple data languages is valued in the job market.

Section 4: AI Reflection

I am surprised how easy it is to use AI to extract information from a dataset. I did run into a small problem with the prompt and it gave me an incorrect answer at the beginning. For relatively small datasets, it is possible to check that the AI has performed the task correctly by manual review. However, I am not sure how this could be done for very large datasets.
